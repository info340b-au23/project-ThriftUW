UW Thrift App

Problem Description (Problem/Data Domain)

Our proposed project focuses on creating an online thrift store & social media integration tailored for students at the University of Washington. The data we will present can be extracted from other sources of online fashion sellers local to students(Facebook Marketplace). Using this information, we can mimic how the website will function, focusing on personalized profiles that will sell the items we gathered. The general topic of interest is sustainable fashion and accessible thrift shopping for college students.

Problems that need addressing

  • Access to affordable fashion: College students often face financial constraints and are constantly seeking affordable yet stylish clothing options. Thrift shopping is the best alternative for a budget-friendly and sustainable option but finding the best thrift store items can be challenging.
  • Community and inspiration: It helps students to find style inspiration from the community around them. They want to see what their peers are wearing and discover unique clothing while promoting sustainable fashion practices. RSOs such as FittedUW can benefit from having an online fashion community.
  • Convenience and Financial Independence: Shopping at multiple thrift stores can be time-consuming and expensive depending on distances travelled. Developing a thrift-centric site that filters out the need to spend hours outside searching for good apparel/deals can help streamline the overall process. This site would also provide entrepreneurial opportunities for UW students, allowing them to sell their items and immediately support the thrifting community through their own purchases or their own thrifting businesses.
  • Sustainability: Along with the trend of thrifting due to social media influences, purchasing second-hand clothing tends to be a more sustainable option. It reduces environmental impact, as well as fulfills people's desires to own luxury items at a fraction of the cost.

Sites such as Depop and Pinterest and online vendors/influencers on social media apps already exist. However, the platforms lack a localized and community-centered approach for UW students. Also, most of these generic online thrift stores lack features that help students discover thrift stores or sellers local to the area.

This proposed website aims to bridge the gap by offering a dedicated platform where students can create profiles, post pictures of outfits, list clothing items for sale, and follow others in the UW community. The platform will also provide features to tag clothing with locations where to find products. The goal is to address the specific needs of UW students, making thrift shopping more accessible and sustainable to promote fashion culture within the campus community.

Proposed Application (App Description)

The UW thrift app aims to connect one another through creativity and shared interests within the community. College is a time of exploration, discover, and learning. Often times, we express ourselves through our unique styles. We want to provide a platform for students to share their unique selves, as well as discover new ways to express themselves, while keeping within their budgets. On a higher level, we hope to create an impact through our app to promote sustainability, decrease environment impacts and fast fashion. We strive to preserve individuality, while we contribute on improving issues that are bigger than ourselves.

The primary users of this application are University of Washington Students. Those who are intersted in fashion and thrifting would use this application. The group of UW students could be undergrad or graduatuate students either living on campus or off campus. All users come from very diverse background of ethnicity, majors, year, religion and more. Their is definitly positive and negative impacts this application can have on minority groups. One positive is inclusivity. This platform hosts so many diverse senses of style from students that come from very diverse backgrounds. This platform with all empower students to branch out with their style choices. It is a way for students to express themselves through fashion and display their unique individualism with their peers. One negative aspect of this platform could be discrimation or bias amongst users where they can interact with one another in chat sections or comments. Also, the platform should be designed and developed in a manner that those with disabilities can access the platform.

That being said, the features on our platform are catered to user needs. Our target audience are mainly students around the UW area. The main features that are implemented will allow users to post and sell their own clothing, purchase posted clothing items from other users, message other users, and engage with the community by interacting with user posts (different from posts that sell clothing items).

Users will use this web application for a number of reasons. One of them being to buy and sell their clothing to other UW students. Users will be able to list their clothing on the site as well as make purchases on other users' clothing. This is an important part of why our platform is helping UW students as well as the environemnt because it is promoting sustainable fashion and the resuse of clothing. This will be on our marketplace so users can make secure transactions. Second feature is sharing and discovering new fashion. Users have the ability to explore other UW students fashion, clothing, styles, and get inspired from them. With being able to view users profiles, their outfits, discover new brands, thrift shops, filter through top sellers and brands, users have free reign to make their own choices. Finally our last feature is about building a fashion community for UW students. We want students to connect with one another through fashion and style. Hence our platform allows them to follow one another, comment on posts, like, share, and have dicussions. This involves user engagement and having key social features that are present on social media applications. We want to inccorporate likes, sharing, direct messages, following and more!
